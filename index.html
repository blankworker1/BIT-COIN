Guides
01
Open Verify
Go to verify.html on any device with a browser. No app needed.
02
Enter the address
Type or paste the Bitcoin address from the coin. It starts with tb1q... (testnet) or bc1q... (mainnet). In V2, tapping the NFC chip will fill this automatically.
03
Read the results
You will see the current balance, lock status, and how many blocks remain until unlock. A LOCKED status means the funds cannot be moved by anyone — not even the key holder.
04
Confirm the birth certificate
Paste the OP_RETURN TXID from the coin's JSON payload into the birth certificate field and click Look Up. This confirms the coin was properly registered on-chain at creation.
The private key is stored on the NFC tag and is readable by anyone. This is by design — the timelock makes the key irrelevant until 2046. The security is in the lock, not the key.
01
Before you start
You need a Bitcoin wallet connected to Mutinynet (testing) or mainnet. For testing, use Zeus wallet with LDK Node set to Mutinynet. Get free test coins from faucet.mutinynet.com. Serve the files from a local server — open a terminal, navigate to the folder, and run python -m http.server 8080, then open http://localhost:8080/mint.html.
02
Step 01 — Configure
Enter a serial number. Select network. For testing, choose a short lock using Test Mode — +20 blocks expires in about 10 minutes on Mutinynet. Click Generate Coin.
03
Step 02 — Fund the coin
Scan the QR code with your wallet and send the amount you want to lock. This is the face value of the coin. Any amount sent to this address is locked until the unlock date.
04
Step 02b — Fee wallet
Click Generate Fee Wallet. Scan the QR and send 2,000–5,000 sats. This pays the miner fee for the OP_RETURN transaction. The page detects the deposit automatically and fills Step 03.
05
Step 03 — Broadcast birth certificate
Click Broadcast OP_RETURN. This records the coin ID and address permanently on-chain. Wait for the green confirmation dot — on Mutinynet this takes about 30 seconds.
06
Step 04 — Save the payload
Download the JSON file. This contains everything needed to verify and eventually sweep the coin — address, private key, redeem script, and birth TX. Keep it safe.
V1 · V2 Roadmap
V1 (current): JSON payload is written to the NTAG424 chip manually using NFC Tools Pro on Android. The chip UID is not yet linked to the coin ID.

V2 (planned): The minting station reads the chip UID automatically on tap, uses it as the coin ID, and writes the payload back to the chip in the same flow. No separate NFC app needed.

The timelock
Every coin is locked by Bitcoin's CLTV opcode (BIP-65). This is not a password or a service — it is a rule enforced by every node on the Bitcoin network. No transaction spending the coin will be accepted until block 1,951,500, approximately 22 May 2046. The private key is visible. It does not matter. The key is useless until that date.
—
The race
When block 1,951,500 arrives, the lock expires for everyone simultaneously. Anyone who has ever held the coin and copied the key can now broadcast a spending transaction. Bitcoin does not care who they are — it accepts the first valid transaction that reaches the miners. This is the race.
—
RBF — your advantage
The sweep tool pre-signs a transaction before the critical block arrives. The moment the lock expires, it broadcasts immediately. If a competing transaction appears in the mempool, the tool automatically escalates the fee — making your transaction more attractive to miners. This is Replace-by-Fee (BIP-125). The person who prepared wins.
—
How to use the sweep tool in 2046
Open sweep.html. Load your coin JSON file. Enter your destination wallet address. Set your fee strategy. Click Pre-sign, then Arm. Keep the tab open. The tool does the rest.
Why this is secure until 2046
A thief who steals the coin today has the private key. They cannot use it. They must wait 21 years — at which point they have spent two decades storing a coin that may or may not still be in their possession. The timelock turns theft into a 21-year custody problem with no guaranteed outcome. Physical possession in 2046 is the strongest claim.
